      📈 Advanced Performance & Randomness

      Theoretical Max Loss Streak A mathematical prediction of maximum consecutive losses based on your win rate and trade count.

      Consider this a mental safeguard: losses up to this count are statistically normal. Use it to stay calm and follow your rules. If you significantly exceed this, it may be time to review your strategy.             -
      Edge Reliability (t-value) Statistically evaluates if performance is distinct from random chance.

      ✨ > 1.96: Strong statistical edge.
      👀 0 ~ 1.96: Neutral, keep collecting data.
      💡 < 0: Indicates a challenging period. Great time to review trades.             -
      Max Stagnation Maximum consecutive trades without hitting a new equity high. Helps detect edge decay over time. -
      Z-Score Measures the randomness of win/loss streaks.

      📊 ±1.96: Standard random distribution.
      📉 <-1.96: Prone to streaks (trend-following).
      📈 >+1.96: Alternating results (mean-reversion).             -

      🎲 Monte Carlo Simulation Simulates 1,000 equity paths of 500 future trades by randomly sampling past trade results. Measures system robustness.

      Disclaimer on Mathematical Models

      The Kelly Criterion and Monte Carlo simulations in this tool are based on the pure mathematical assumption that past trade results will occur independently with the exact same probabilities in the future.

      They do not account for external risks such as sudden market regime shifts, worsened slippage, or liquidity crunches. They DO NOT guarantee future profits or principal. Please use them strictly at your own risk as a theoretical benchmark based on historical data.
